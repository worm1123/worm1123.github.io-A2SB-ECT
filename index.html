A2SB-ECT: high-fidelity music bandwidth expansion
Accelerated by Easy Consistency Tuning

High-fidelity music bandwidth expansion aims to recover the full broadband spectrum from limited low-frequency audio. Although Schrödinger Bridge (SB)-based generative models can reconstruct high-frequency content with high quality, their multi-step sampling delay limits real-time deployment. This work proposes A2SB-ECT, which introduces the Easy Consistency Tuning (ECT) framework into Schrödinger Bridge music restoration models for the first time. Through strict single-step (NFE=1) inference, it achieves end-to-end 44.1 kHz studio-grade music restoration without a codec. In addition, we designed a phase-aware prior constraint module to improve the model's phase prediction capability. Experiments on our test set demonstrate that A2SB-ECT outperforms multi-step SB baselines in both spectral quality and subjective perception scores under single-step inference, while meeting real-time processing requirements.
